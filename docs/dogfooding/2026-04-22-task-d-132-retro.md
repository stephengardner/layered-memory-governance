## Position + Counter rationale (reconstructed from stdout + atom summaries)

Full Position/Counter text was written to the in-memory AtomStore and
lost at process exit. Only the final Decision is serialized to stdout.
This is a gap: for rigorous post-mortem we would need a file-backed
AtomStore or an argv flag to dump all atoms. Capturing as a canon-debt
observation below.

From the Decision's arbitrationTrace + the CTO's phrasing, the
structure was:
- code-author's position (winning): argv-array + `shell:false`,
  failing-first test, 2-principal review.
- CTO's position (losing): similar conclusion (drop shell:true) but
  framed without the failing-first discipline or the two-principal
  governance note.
- code-author's counter-atom: added the governance framing that
  the CTO's cut missed (two-principal approve, code-author cannot
  self-merge).
- Source-rank arbitration picked the code-author position with the
  counter's refinement folded in.

This is governance working - the lower-rank principal's objection
won on substance, not hierarchy.

## What surprised

- **The task-#132 "bug" doesn't exist.** Main HEAD has had the fix
  since 2026-04-21. The prompt was asking the org to rediscover prior
  work. That is actually a good test - the org handled it sanely - but
  the task brief should flag it.
- **Execute mode is the default in boot.mjs.** Omit the flag, you get
  execute. Given that execute is currently broken end-to-end at the
  fence, this is a harmless UX trap today; it becomes a real footgun
  once the remaining host pieces land. (Addressed by the safety flip
  committed alongside this doc.)
- **Deliberation LLM (CLI client / `anthropic`) and executor LLM
  (`host.llm`, `MemoryLLM`) are SEPARATE injection points.** Real
  deliberation + stub executor is the current steady state. A future
  adapter needs to unify or at least compose them.
- **Position/Counter atoms don't round-trip to stdout.** Only Decision
  + execution outcome reach the operator; the substance of the
  arbitration is in-memory-only. That's a diagnostic blind spot.
